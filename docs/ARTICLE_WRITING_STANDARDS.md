# Article Writing Standards

This document defines the strict standards for all public articles in the Knowledge Base. It supplements the [AGENT_GUIDE.md](./AGENT_GUIDE.md).

All content must be suitable for a public audience on the open internet.

## Core Principle

**Articles may contain only objective, generalizable facts and established knowledge.**

Every sentence must be defensible as a statement of mechanism, observed pattern, research finding, or neutral framework — without personal framing, emotional coloring, or direct address to the reader.

## What Is Allowed

- Mechanisms and models (e.g., "Blood glucose can be modeled as a pool where intake and uptake interact.")
- Functional frameworks and taxonomies (e.g., clear role definitions for components in a system).
- Patterns documented in research or widely observed practice (e.g., "Behavioral finance literature describes recency bias as...")
- Data, tables, and quantitative relationships from public sources.
- Neutral checklists and evaluation frameworks presented as "considerations" or "reference questions."
- Sources and citations.
- Calm, progressive explanatory structure: concepts → mechanisms → implications → practical structures → sources.

## What Is Strictly Prohibited

- Any use of "you", "your", "I", "we", or direct reader address.
- Subjective emotions or value judgments ("anxiety", "fatigue", "frustrating", "important", "truly matters", "real cost to you").
- Prescriptive or advisory language ("you should", "it is better to", "must", "recommended", "the right way").
- Personal narratives, realizations, or stories ("I learned that...", "after experiencing X...", "in my case...").
- Privacy-sensitive content: specific holdings, allocation percentages, rebalancing actions, personal decisions, timelines, companies, locations, or life events.
- Motivational or therapeutic tone ("save your attention for what really matters", "become a better person", "compatible with your true nature").
- "Common sense" claims presented as universal truth without grounding.
- Second-person hypotheticals that simulate personal advice.

## Language and Tone Guidelines

| Instead of (Subjective)                  | Use (Objective)                                      |
|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| "You should ask yourself..."            | "Key evaluation questions include..."                |
| "This often causes anxiety and fatigue" | "This pattern is associated with increased monitoring costs and decision resource consumption." |
| "The real problem is..."                | "One commonly observed issue is..."                  |
| "You need a system that works for your real personality" | "Systems can be designed to account for documented behavioral tendencies." |
| "This is the most important thing"      | "This factor has significant impact on..."           |
| "I realized that..."                    | "Research and practice indicate that..."             |
| "Make sure you don't..."                | "A common failure mode is..."                        |

Use third-person or impersonal constructions:
- "Individuals often..."
- "Portfolios with frequent ad-hoc changes..."
- "Studies show..."
- "A systems approach involves..."
- "This can result in..."

## Privacy and Generalization Rules

When any content originates from personal documents, diaries, experiences, or notes:

- Strip **all** personal context.
- Present only the abstracted mechanism, principle, or framework.
- Never reference the source experience, even obliquely.
- Treat the article as if it could be read by anyone, anywhere, at any time.

Existing privacy rules in AGENT_GUIDE.md (itinerary stripping, time-sensitive information, etc.) remain in force and are extended by the above.

## Structural Requirements

- Follow the progressive explanatory model used in existing articles (see blood-glucose.html for reference).
- Use headings, tables, lists, and callout notes (`.note`) for clarity.
- Every article must include a table of contents linking to the top-level
  sections.
- Interactive elements are permitted only when they illustrate objective relationships (not when they deliver advice).
- Every article must have matching Simplified Chinese and English versions.
- End with a neutral "Sources and Notes" section that includes disclaimers.

## Verification Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] No "you/your/I/we" directed at the reader.
- [ ] No emotional or motivational language.
- [ ] No personal stories, decisions, or data.
- [ ] All claims are either mechanisms, documented patterns, or clearly sourced.
- [ ] Language is neutral and factual throughout (including notes, tables, tool output, and footer).
- [ ] Bilingual versions are consistent in meaning and tone.
- [ ] Article reads as a standalone explanatory resource, not as guidance derived from any individual's life.

## Relationship to AGENT_GUIDE.md

This document takes precedence on matters of article **content tone and factual standards**. The AGENT_GUIDE.md continues to govern overall site architecture, visual design, bilingual requirements, build process, and high-level privacy rules for personal-source material.

Update this document whenever the factual or tonal standards are refined.
